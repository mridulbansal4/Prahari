## 3. The Compliance Gap — P-101B OISD Periodic Inspection

**Item 1 of the matrix is overdue and is the principal finding of this register.**

The OISD periodic inspection of the rotating equipment **P-101B** is required at an interval of **12 months**. The **last recorded inspection was performed on 2019-03-11**. It was therefore due again on **2020-03-10**.

As at the register date of **2026-07-21**, this inspection is **more than seven years overdue**. Six successive annual inspections (those due in 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025 and 2026) have not been performed and no deferral concession, risk assessment or written extension of interval exists on file for any of them.

**P-101B has remained in service throughout this period**, alternating duty with P-101A. It has not been withdrawn from service, and no compensating inspection regime was formally substituted.

This is an unambiguous non-compliance. It is not a documentation lag: no inspection report, no inspector's field notes, no photographic record, no thickness survey and no internal examination record exists for P-101B for any date after 2019-03-11. The absence is of the inspection itself, not merely of the paperwork.

---

## 4. Consequences of the Gap

**Regulatory exposure.** P-101B is boiler feed service on a plant operating under an OISD-compliant safety management framework. An external safety audit or an OISD surveillance visit reviewing the inspection register would classify a seven-year lapse on a critical rotating machine as a major non-conformity. Under the Factories Act the manager and the occupier carry a personal statutory duty regarding the safe condition of plant; an Inspector of Factories finding that a machine in continuous service has gone seven years without its scheduled examination is entitled to issue an improvement notice, and in the event of an incident the lapse becomes evidence in any prosecution. Because the machine handles hot water above its atmospheric boiling point, a containment failure is a credible severe-injury scenario, not a theoretical one.

**Insurance exposure.** The plant's machinery breakdown and business interruption cover is written on the basis that statutory and standard-mandated inspections are current. A claim arising from a P-101B failure could be resisted on the grounds that the inspection warranty was breached. This exposure is present now and grows with each month the gap remains open.

**Technical risk.** Seven years without a formal internal inspection means there is no verified record of casing wear-ring clearances, no confirmed erosion or cavitation-damage assessment on the impellers and diffusers, no shaft condition assessment beyond what has been incidentally observed during corrective seal work, and no verified check of the balance drum condition. Wear-ring clearance opening in a multistage feed pump degrades efficiency and increases rotor instability progressively and silently — the vibration trend on VE-101B may remain within its evaluation zone while internal clearances drift well outside the OEM limits. The condition-monitoring data in Item 2 is genuinely current and genuinely useful, but it is not a substitute for the internal inspection and must not be presented as one.

**Register integrity.** Because the Factories Act machinery examination register (Item 12) draws on the same underlying inspection evidence, its P-101B entry has been carried forward against a 2019 basis. The register is therefore formally up to date but materially incomplete. This must be corrected in the same remediation cycle, and the correction must be recorded as a correction, not as a silent overwrite.

**Evidence missing:**

- Any OISD periodic inspection report for P-101B for 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025 or 2026.
- Any written deferral, concession, interval-extension approval or compensating-measures risk assessment covering the missed inspections.

This should be verified rather than assumed, and the same failure mode should be checked across the rest of the rotating equipment population.

## 6. Paraphrased Summary of the Applicable Obligations

The following are site summaries only. The published standards and statutes are authoritative.

**OISD-GDN-134 (inspection of rotating equipment)** sets out a framework for the periodic inspection and condition monitoring of pumps, compressors and drivers in the hydrocarbon and allied process industries. It establishes that rotating machines be assigned inspection intervals based on criticality and service, that condition-monitoring parameters be trended, that internal inspections be performed at defined intervals with clearances measured against OEM limits, and that inspection findings and measurements be recorded in a retrievable equipment history. It also expects that overdue inspections be visible to management and formally dispositioned rather than silently carried.

**OISD-STD-105 (work permit system)** governs the classification, issue, validity, renewal and closure of work permits, including gas testing and the responsibilities of issuing and receiving authorities.

**OISD-STD-137 (pressure relieving devices)** establishes inspection and set-pressure testing intervals for relief devices, applicable here to the V-201 relief arrangement.

**Factories Act, 1948, section 31** requires that where any part of plant or machinery is operated at a pressure above atmospheric, effective measures be taken to ensure that the safe working pressure is not exceeded, and that such plant be examined and recorded as prescribed under the State Factories Rules. **Sections 21 and 22** address the fencing of machinery and restrictions on work on machinery in motion. The Act obliges the occupier and manager to maintain the prescribed registers of examination and to produce them to the Inspector of Factories on demand.

**PESO**, administering the Explosives Act 1884, the Petroleum Act 1934 and the associated Rules including SMPV(U), requires periodic external and internal examination and hydrostatic testing of covered pressure vessels by a competent person at the intervals prescribed in the applicable Rules, with certificates retained and produced on demand, and requires prior approval for modification to licensed equipment.

## 8. Escalation Statement

**P-101B is operating with its mandatory OISD periodic inspection overdue by more than seven years. The required interval is 12 months.
