# Project Context

Prahari is being built as the solution for ET Hackathon Problem Statement 8.

## Problem Statement

Industrial organizations generate enormous volumes of engineering documents, maintenance records, operational procedures, inspection reports, asset information, incident reports, and historical knowledge.

This information is fragmented across multiple systems, documents, departments, and teams, making it difficult to retrieve, reason over, and use for operational decision-making.

Engineers spend significant time searching for information instead of solving problems, while valuable organizational knowledge is lost due to personnel changes, siloed systems, and disconnected workflows.

The challenge is not simply document search—it is transforming fragmented industrial knowledge into actionable organizational intelligence that improves operational efficiency, decision quality, and institutional memory.

## Vision

Prahari is an Industrial Decision Intelligence Operating System.

Rather than functioning as a chatbot or document search engine, Prahari continuously learns from organizational knowledge, connects information across domains, preserves engineering decision history, and assists teams in making faster, more informed operational decisions.

## Objectives

- Build a unified industrial intelligence platform.
- Preserve organizational knowledge.
- Capture and evolve engineering decisions.
- Connect structured and unstructured industrial data.
- Reduce knowledge retrieval time.
- Improve operational decision quality.
- Create a continuously learning organizational memory.

## Success Criteria

The platform should demonstrate measurable improvements in:

- Knowledge retrieval
- Cross-document reasoning
- Decision support
- Operational efficiency
- Knowledge preservation
- Explainability
- Trust
- Enterprise readiness

## Target Users

- Plant Engineers
- Maintenance Engineers
- Reliability Engineers
- Operations Teams
- EPC Organizations
- Asset Managers
- Plant Supervisors
- Engineering Leadership
- Industrial Knowledge Workers

## Guiding Principle

Every feature implemented in Prahari should contribute to transforming fragmented industrial knowledge into actionable organizational intelligence.

Features that do not support this objective should not be added unless explicitly approved.
